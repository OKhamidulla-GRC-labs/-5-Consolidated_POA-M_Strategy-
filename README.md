Building Cyber Resilience: A Consolidated POA&M Strategy Mapped to Industry-Leading Frameworks & Benchmarks

Just completed a comprehensive Plan of Action & Milestones (POA&M) assessment identifying 25 security weaknesses across our organization's 2026 assessment cycle. Here's a summary of findings and the path forward:

Scope of Findings
Weaknesses span 8 critical domains:
→ Endpoint Security
→ Identity & Access Management
→ Cloud Services
→ Network Infrastructure
→ Application Security
→ Data Privacy
→ Governance
→ Disaster Recovery
 Risk Posture
A significant concentration of High and Critical severity issues demand immediate attention, including:
• MFA bypass vulnerabilities for Office 365 accounts
• Unsupported and vulnerable software components still in production
• Unmasked production data exposed in development environments
• Continued use of insecure legacy protocols (e.g., SMBv1)
These pose direct threats to confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
 Root Cause Patterns
Recurring themes include legacy systems lagging behind modern security requirements, governance gaps (missed review cycles, insufficient automation), and architectural shortcomings such as inadequate network segmentation, improper backup storage design, and insufficient DR planning.
 Remediation Timeline
• Critical items (MFA bypass, CI/CD dependency-confusion risks, SMBv1 elimination) → Early May–June 2026
• High-risk items (backup architecture, IoT network segmentation, log retention expansion) → Mid-2026
• Medium-risk items → Extended through October 2026

Current Status
A substantial number of items remain in Open, Planned, or Not Started states. A structured governance model — including a recurring POA&M review board — is being established to maintain schedule discipline and proactive resource allocation.

 Strategic Approach
We're adopting a risk-based remediation model:
Immediate focus on direct exploitation and compliance exposure
Long-term investment in legacy modernization
Implementation of zero-trust principles Enhancement of disaster recovery capabilities
